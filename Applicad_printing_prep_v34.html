Decision Guide

When to Use Each

Fuse 1+ 30W — Best For

Rapid Prototyping & Small Batches
The Fuse 1+ excels when you need fast turnaround on smaller parts, want full in-house control, or are iterating on a design. The modular chamber means you can start a new print while the previous one cools — keeping throughput high without a large footprint.
Prototyping Small batch (<50 parts) Nylon 12 Fast iteration Parts <160 mm In-house workflow

Stratasys H350 — Best For

High-Volume Production Runs
The H350 is the right choice when volume, consistency and cost-per-part matter most. SAF™ Big Wave™ powder deposition delivers a uniform thermal experience across the full 315 × 208 mm bed — meaning part-to-part variation is minimal even at the edges.
Production runs High volume (50+ parts) Nylon 12 Cost per part focus Parts up to 315 mm Certification needed

From Print to Part

Post-print Workflow

SLS parts don't come out print-ready. Here's what happens after the build chamber finishes sintering.

1
🌡
Cooldown Phase
The build chamber cools naturally inside the printer. Forced cooling risks warping — typical cooldown is 50–75% of print time.
2
📤
Chamber Removal
On the Fuse 1+, the modular chamber is swapped out while a new build starts. On the H350, the full bed assembly is transferred to the post-processing unit.
3
🌬
Depowdering
Loose powder is removed by hand, vacuum, or the Fuse Blast (automated). Unsintered powder is sieved and returned to the hopper for reuse.
4
🔍
Inspection & QC
Parts are inspected dimensionally. Warping, sink marks, or surface defects are noted. Critical parts may be measured against a CAD nominal.
5
✨
Surface Finishing
Parts are bead-blasted, tumble-polished, dyed, or coated depending on aesthetics and functional requirements.
6
📋
Documentation
Build data, material lot, refresh rate and part count are logged. The H350's GrabCAD Server can automate this for quality certification workflows.
